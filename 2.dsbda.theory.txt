STEP 7 — OUTLIER DETECTION

MOST IMPORTANT SECTION.

WHAT IS OUTLIER?

Value far away from normal data.

Example:

20,21,22,19,18,500

500 = outlier.

WHY DANGEROUS?

Outliers:

distort mean,
affect graphs,
reduce ML accuracy.
IQR METHOD
WHAT IS QUARTILE?

Quartiles divide data into 4 parts.

Q1

25th percentile.

Q2

Median.

Q3

75th percentile.

THIS LINE
Q1 = df[col].quantile(0.25)

finds:

first quartile
THIS LINE
Q3 = df[col].quantile(0.75)

finds:

third quartile
WHAT IS IQR?

Formula:

IQR=Q3−Q1

Measures spread of middle 50% data.

OUTLIER LIMITS

Lower Bound:

Q1−1.5×IQR

Upper Bound:

Q3+1.5×IQR

Outside values = outliers.

THIS LINE
outliers = df[(df[col] < lower) | (df[col] > upper)]

finds all outlier rows.

WHAT IS | ?

Logical OR.

STEP 8 — WINSORIZATION

MOST IMPORTANT VIVA QUESTION.

WHAT IS WINSORIZATION?

Instead of deleting outliers:

cap them to boundary values.
WHY BETTER?

Because:

preserves dataset size,
avoids data loss.
THIS LINE
np.where()

works like:

conditional replacement
STRUCTURE
np.where(condition, true_value, false_value)
HERE
np.where(df[col] > upper, upper, df[col])

means:

if value > upper limit
→ replace with upper limit
otherwise keep original.

STEP 10 — LOG TRANSFORMATION

WHAT IS LOG?

Mathematical logarithm.

Compresses large values.

WHY LOG TRANSFORMATION USED?

To:

reduce skewness,
reduce extreme effect,
make data more normal.
THIS LINE
df['Maths_log'] = np.log(df['Maths'] + 1)

creates transformed column.

WHY +1?

Because:

log(0) is undefined

Adding 1 avoids log(0).

WHAT IS SKEWNESS?

Measure of asymmetry in distribution.

TYPES
Type	Meaning
positive skew	long right tail
negative skew	long left tail
zero skew	symmetric
THIS LINE

calculates skewness.

INTERPRETATION
Skewness	Meaning
0	normal
>0	right skew
<0	left skew
