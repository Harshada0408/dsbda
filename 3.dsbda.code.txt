# theory

# descriptive statistics

descriptive statistics are statistical methods used to summarize and describe important characteristics of data.

they help in:

* understanding dataset distribution
* identifying patterns
* comparing groups
* summarizing numerical data

---

# measures of central tendency

measures of central tendency represent the central or average value of dataset.

types:

* mean
* median
* mode

---

# mean

mean is the arithmetic average of values.

formula:

Mean = (∑x)/n

where:

* x = observations
* n = total number of observations

---

# median

median is the middle value when data is arranged in order.

if number of observations is even:
median is average of two middle values.

---

# minimum and maximum

* minimum = smallest value
* maximum = largest value

these help identify range of dataset.

---

# standard deviation

standard deviation measures spread of data around mean.

small standard deviation:

* values are close to mean

large standard deviation:

* values are spread out

formula:

σ= sqrt((∑(x−μ)^2​)/n)
​

---

# percentile

percentile indicates percentage of observations below a given value.

examples:

* 25th percentile
* 50th percentile
* 75th percentile

50th percentile is also called median.

---

in this practical:

species categories:
